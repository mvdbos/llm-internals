Mission

Learn enough about model dataflow, numeric representation, runtime state, and evaluation to make a scoped, evidence-backed quantization decision.

What learners will be able to do

  1. Distinguish learned parameters, temporary activations, and architecture-specific retained runtime state.
  2. Explain a quantization round trip—including mapping, metadata, reconstruction, and error—without treating a bit width as a complete format.
  3. Classify checkpoints, artifacts, tensor encodings, recipes, inference engines, backends, kernels, and hardware before comparing candidates.
  4. Calculate weight and retained-state estimates with explicit units, then verify the real memory boundary.
  5. Design a matched workload evaluation and choose among measured Pareto points without inventing a universal ranking.

Evidence standard

The course separates canonical implementation facts, measured observations, pedagogical examples, and causal hypotheses. A format suffix, file size, perplexity change, or step count does not by itself establish downstream quality. Quantitative claims name their model/artifact scope, revision, metric, and measurement boundary.

Current publication boundary

Lessons 1–4 and the reference material teach the independent foundations and evaluation method. The final decision capstone and controlled-comparison results are deferred until the preregistered paired trials are resumed and pass their evidence gate. The course does not substitute historical or pilot observations for those results.
